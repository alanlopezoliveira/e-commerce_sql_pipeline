E-commerce SQL Pipeline (MySQL)
A reproducible SQL analytics pipeline for a small e-commerce dataset. Demonstrates best practices in data modeling, cleaning, analysis, and advanced SQL features.
View on GitHub
Dataset Overview
Four raw CSV files power the pipeline, covering countries, customers, products, and sales transactions.
country_raw.csv
Country codes and names (includes duplicates).
customer_raw.csv
Customer ID, name, gender, birthdate, country.
product_raw.csv
Product catalog: ID, name, category, price.
sales_raw.csv
Transactions: customer, product, date, quantity, amount.
Business Questions Answered
The pipeline is designed to answer key analytical questions across revenue, products, and geography.
Monthly Revenue
How does revenue evolve over time?
Top Products & Customers
Who and what drives the most sales?
Average Ticket
What is the average ticket by product category?
Zero-Sales Products
Which products have no sales at all?
Geo & Time Breakdown
How do sales break down by country and month?
All queries are documented in sql/05_analysis_queries.sql (8+ queries).
Project Structure
data/
Raw CSV files — do not edit.
01_schema.sql
Creates all staging and core tables.
02_load_staging.sql
Data quality checks after CSV import.
03_transform_core.sql
Cleans and loads data into core tables.
04_semantic_views.sql
Business views for analysis.
05–07 SQL Files
Analysis queries, quality checks, and advanced SQL.
How to Reproduce
Follow these 8 steps to run the full pipeline in MySQL Workbench, DBeaver, or CLI.
The pipeline is fully sequential — each step builds on the previous. All cleaning is done in SQL; CSVs are imported as-is.
Findings & Data Quality
Issues Found
  • Duplicate country codes in country_raw.csv
  • Some customers have missing country codes or malformed birthdates
  • Invalid or missing numeric fields in products and sales — filtered during transformation
